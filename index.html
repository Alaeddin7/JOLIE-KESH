Takshita

A Moroccan traditional women's garment that is worn for celebrations, particularly weddings. It is composed of two pieces, a dress as a first layer called "Tahtia", often of fine but not ornately decorated fabric, and a more elaborate second layer or over-dress that often buttons up the front using the traditional sfifa and akaad closures and it is called "Dfina". And our Takshitas are fashionable.

Cosmetics

The fruits are picked by hand and dried in the sun for a few weeks.

2. Kernel roasting.

The kernels are next gently toasted for a few minutes on clay plates. Then, the roasted kernels are ground.

3. Peeling fruits.

After drying, the dried peel is carefully removed, resulting in “argan nuts.”

4. Extraction of oil.

After peeling the fruits, press extraction is used to acquire the oil.

5. Cracking nuts.

The argan nuts are cracked open, and the kernels are picked and gathered.
